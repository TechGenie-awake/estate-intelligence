# Government Housing Schemes and Tax Benefits in India

## Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY)
PMAY is the flagship government scheme providing affordable housing to Economically Weaker Sections (EWS), Low-Income Groups (LIG), and Middle-Income Groups (MIG). Under PMAY-Urban, eligible beneficiaries receive an interest subsidy on home loans through the Credit Linked Subsidy Scheme (CLSS):
- EWS/LIG (annual income up to ₹6 lakh): Subsidy of 6.5% on loans up to ₹6 lakh, max benefit ₹2.67 lakh.
- MIG-I (income ₹6-12 lakh): Subsidy of 4% on loans up to ₹9 lakh, max benefit ₹2.35 lakh.
- MIG-II (income ₹12-18 lakh): Subsidy of 3% on loans up to ₹12 lakh, max benefit ₹2.30 lakh.

PMAY is typically applicable to properties priced under ₹45 lakh with carpet area limits (30 sqm EWS, 60 sqm LIG, 160 sqm MIG-I, 200 sqm MIG-II).

## Income Tax Deductions on Home Loan
Under the Income Tax Act, home loan borrowers can claim:
- Section 80C: Up to ₹1.5 lakh deduction on principal repayment.
- Section 24(b): Up to ₹2 lakh deduction on interest paid for self-occupied property.
- Section 80EEA: Additional ₹1.5 lakh deduction on interest for first-time buyers (property value up to ₹45 lakh).
- Section 80EE: Up to ₹50,000 additional deduction (for loans sanctioned between specific periods).

## GST on Real Estate
- Under-construction residential property: 5% GST (1% for affordable housing under ₹45 lakh).
- Ready-to-move-in property: No GST.
- Commercial property: 12% GST.

## RERA (Real Estate Regulatory Authority)
The Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 mandates registration of all residential and commercial projects over 500 sqm or 8 units with the state RERA authority. Buyers should always verify:
- RERA registration number of the project
- Promoter's past project delivery history
- Carpet area (not super built-up) as mandated by RERA

## Stamp Duty and Registration
Stamp duty varies by state: typically 4-8% of property value. Maharashtra charges 5-6%, Karnataka 5%, Tamil Nadu 7%, Delhi 4-6%. Registration fees are usually 1% of property value. Some states offer 1% concession for female buyers.
