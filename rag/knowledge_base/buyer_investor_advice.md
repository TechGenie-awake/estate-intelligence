# Buyer and Investor Advisory Notes for Indian Real Estate

## Due Diligence Checklist Before Buying
Before purchasing any residential property in India, buyers should verify:
1. Clear title and mother deed (chain of ownership for last 30 years).
2. RERA registration of the project (for new launches and ongoing projects).
3. Approved building plan from local municipal authority.
4. Commencement Certificate (CC) and Occupancy Certificate (OC) for the project.
5. Encumbrance Certificate for the last 15 years.
6. No Objection Certificates (NOCs) from utilities, fire department, and environmental clearance where applicable.
7. Property tax receipts for the last 3-5 years.

## When the ML-Predicted Price Seems High
If the algorithmic price estimate is higher than comparable listings in the area, the property may have premium features that the market has not yet priced in (new metro line, upcoming school, recent renovation). Alternatively, the estimate may be optimistic. Verify by:
- Checking 5-10 comparable sold transactions (not just listings) in the same micro-market.
- Getting at least 2 independent broker valuations.
- Reviewing circle rate / ready reckoner rate set by the state government.

## When the ML-Predicted Price Seems Low
If the estimate is below asking price or perceived market value, the property may be undervalued due to:
- Pending legal issues or unclear title.
- Negative neighborhood factors (flood-prone, low water table, poor drainage).
- Upcoming adverse infrastructure (elevated highway, garbage facility, high-tension lines).
- Builder reputation or delivery delays.
Always investigate why before treating it as a bargain.

## Buyer Recommendations by Price Band
- Under ₹45 lakh: Explore PMAY CLSS subsidy; prioritize RERA-registered affordable housing.
- ₹45 lakh-₹1 crore: Focus on tier-1 suburbs or tier-2 city centers; verify connectivity.
- ₹1-₹2 crore: Look for gated communities with amenities in established neighborhoods.
- Above ₹2 crore: Prioritize prime/preferred areas (prefarea = Yes); ensure exit liquidity.

## Investor Recommendations
- Rental yield investors: Target Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Pune 2-3BHK units near IT corridors.
- Capital appreciation: Emerging suburbs with announced infrastructure (metro Phase 2, ring roads).
- Short-term flips: High risk; liquidity in Indian residential market is slow (90-180 days to sell).
- Diversification: Do not allocate more than 40-50% of investible net worth to a single property.

## Negotiation Benchmarks
- On asking price, buyers typically negotiate 5-12% discount in tier-1 metros.
- Ready-to-move flats in distress sales: 10-18% discount possible.
- New launches: Developers often offer 4-6% discount plus waived registration or GST absorbed during festive seasons (Diwali, Gudi Padwa, Akshaya Tritiya).

## Red Flags to Avoid
- Properties without clear OC (Occupancy Certificate) — indicates unauthorized construction.
- Rates significantly below market (verify on RERA and circle rate portals).
- Builders with multiple pending RERA complaints.
- Projects with more than 50% inventory unsold after 2 years — indicates demand issues.
