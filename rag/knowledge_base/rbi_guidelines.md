# RBI Guidelines for Home Loans and Real Estate Financing (India)

## Repo Rate and Home Loan Interest Rates
The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) sets the repo rate, which directly influences home loan interest rates offered by commercial banks. As of 2024-25, the RBI has maintained the repo rate at 6.5%. Home loan interest rates offered by major Indian banks (SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis, Kotak) typically range between 8.5% and 9.5% p.a. for salaried borrowers with good credit scores (CIBIL 750+).

## Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio Caps
As per RBI regulations, the maximum Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio for home loans is:
- Loans up to ₹30 lakh: up to 90% LTV (borrower contributes 10% down payment)
- Loans from ₹30 lakh to ₹75 lakh: up to 80% LTV (borrower contributes 20% down payment)
- Loans above ₹75 lakh: up to 75% LTV (borrower contributes 25% down payment)

## Priority Sector Lending (PSL)
Housing loans up to ₹35 lakh in metropolitan cities (population > 10 lakh) and ₹25 lakh in other cities qualify as Priority Sector Lending, making them eligible for preferential interest rates and faster processing.

## External Benchmark Lending Rate (EBLR)
Since October 2019, all new floating-rate home loans from scheduled commercial banks are linked to an external benchmark, most commonly the RBI repo rate. This means borrower rates adjust every 3 months as repo rate changes.

## Foreclosure and Prepayment Charges
The RBI has prohibited banks from charging foreclosure or prepayment penalties on floating-rate home loans taken by individual borrowers. Fixed-rate loans may attract charges of 2-4%.

## Credit Score Requirements
Most banks require a minimum CIBIL score of 700-750 for best rates. Scores below 650 often result in loan rejection or rates 1-2% higher than market.
