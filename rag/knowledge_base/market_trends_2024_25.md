# Indian Real Estate Market Trends 2024-25

## Tier-1 City Price Appreciation
The Indian residential real estate market has witnessed strong growth in 2024-25. Tier-1 cities (Mumbai, Delhi NCR, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Chennai, Pune, Kolkata) have reported year-on-year price appreciation ranging between 8% and 12%. Hyderabad and Bengaluru lead appreciation with ~12% growth, driven by IT sector expansion and infrastructure development.

## Tier-2 City Emergence
Tier-2 cities such as Coimbatore, Indore, Jaipur, Lucknow, Chandigarh, and Surat are emerging as high-growth residential markets. These cities have seen 6-10% YoY price appreciation as work-from-home policies redistribute housing demand.

## Residential Demand Patterns
- 3BHK units dominate demand in tier-1 urban markets, accounting for ~45% of new bookings.
- 2BHK units remain the most popular configuration in tier-2 and tier-3 cities.
- Demand for larger 4BHK and villa-style homes has surged post-pandemic, especially in suburbs of Bengaluru, Hyderabad, and Pune.
- Affordable housing (under ₹45 lakh) comprises roughly 30% of total residential sales volume.

## Premium Features Driving Price
Properties with the following features command a 15-25% premium over comparable base properties:
- Metro or highway connectivity within 2 km
- Gated community with 24x7 security
- Modular kitchen and branded fittings
- Dedicated parking (stilt or covered)
- Air conditioning (pre-installed)
- Preferred/prime area (CBD or established residential hubs)
- Balcony with unobstructed view

## Rental Yield
Average rental yields in Indian metros range between 2.5% and 4.0% annually:
- Mumbai: 2.5-3.0%
- Bengaluru: 3.5-4.0%
- Hyderabad: 3.5-4.0%
- Delhi NCR: 2.5-3.5%
- Pune: 3.0-3.5%

## Under-Construction vs Ready-to-Move
Ready-to-move properties typically command a 5-10% premium over under-construction units due to elimination of construction delay risk and immediate GST exemption (5% GST on under-construction vs 0% on RTM).

## Furnishing Impact
- Unfurnished units: base price.
- Semi-furnished: 3-7% premium.
- Fully furnished: 8-15% premium, though actual resale value of furniture depreciates quickly.
